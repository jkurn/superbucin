# Game Development: Zero-Budget Resource Guide

The excuse "I have no money, I can't make a game" is no longer valid. From 2D sprites to 3D models and professional audio, the barrier to entry has never been lower. Most of these resources are **CC0 licensed**, meaning they are free for commercial use — often without requiring attribution or registration.

---

## The All-in-One Essentials

### [Kenney.nl](https://kenney.nl)

Think of this as the IKEA of indie game development.

- **Assets:** 40,000+ items (2D sprites, 3D models, UI, fonts, SFX)
- **License:** CC0 (Public Domain)
- **Best for:** Game jams and rapid prototyping. No registration required.

---

## 3D Models & Environments

| Resource | Best For | License |
|----------|----------|---------|
| [Quaternius](https://quaternius.com) | High-quality low-poly characters, animals, and vehicles | CC0 |
| [Poly Haven](https://polyhaven.com) | Photorealistic HDRIs, textures, and 3D models | CC0 |
| [Sketchfab](https://sketchfab.com) | Massive variety (use the "Downloadable" filter) | Varies |
| [Mixamo](https://mixamo.com) | Automated character rigging and thousands of animations | Free |

---

## Textures & Pixel Art

- **[AmbientCG](https://ambientcg.com)** — Over 2,000 PBR materials (Albedo, Normal, Roughness) up to 8K resolution. (CC0)
- **[Piskel](https://www.piskelapp.com)** — Free, browser-based tool for creating pixel art and sprites.
- **[Aseprite](https://www.aseprite.org)** — The industry standard for pixel art. The executable is paid, but you can compile the source code from GitHub for free.

---

## Sound & Music

- **[Freesound.org](https://freesound.org)** — Huge community-driven archive of sound effects.
- **[Mixkit](https://mixkit.co)** — High-quality royalty-free sound effects and music tracks.
- **[Zapsplat](https://www.zapsplat.com)** — Over 119,000 professional sound files.
- **[OpenGameArt](https://opengameart.org)** — A staple for finding open-source game music.

---

## Fonts & UI

Custom fonts can define your game's identity. Check out the curated list on Itch.io:

- [Free Game Fonts on Itch.io](https://itch.io/c/460232/free-game-fonts)
- **Recommended:** `m5x7` and `monogram` for that classic pixel aesthetic.

---

## Final Advice

Making a game isn't about having a massive budget — it's about **time and patience**.

> **The Challenge:** Download an asset pack from Kenney, fire up Godot (or your engine of choice), and try to create something playable within 48 hours.
